## The rise of remote work has unlocked new opportunities for creatives and artists, allowing them to explore the world while maintaining their careers.

However, finding the right city to live and work in as a digital nomad is about more than just affordability and internet speed — it’s about **inspiration, community, and access to artistic spaces**. Whether you're a painter, designer, musician, or writer, choosing the right city can fuel your creativity and help you connect with like-minded professionals.

---

## Where to find creative hubs

If you’re constantly on the move and wondering how to keep your creativity alive, check out our [latest reel on Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/reel/DKh5fWKtzyG/?igsh=ajdyOTM0c3lscWdt). It offers simple yet powerful tips to stay grounded and inspired no matter where you are in the world.

Beyond just finding a place to work, digital nomad creatives look for cities with a **thriving art scene**, dynamic networking opportunities, and **spaces where they can create and showcase** their work.

Historically, places like **Paris** or **Mexico City** have been epicenters of art and culture. These cities, among others, still attract artists with their **world-class museums**, creative neighborhoods, and collective studios.

**Museums, galleries, coworking studios, and creative collectives** are crucial in shaping these environments, offering both practical infrastructure and emotional inspiration.

## 🌍 Top creative destinations for nomads in 2025

- **New York City, USA** — [MoMA](https://www.moma.org/), [NYFA](https://www.nyfa.org/), [Brooklyn Creative League](https://www.brooklyncreativeleague.com/)
- **Berlin, Germany** — [Berlinische Galerie](http://berlinischegalerie.de), [betahaus Berlin](https://www.betahaus.com/)
- **Barcelona, Spain** — [MACBA](https://www.macba.cat/en/), [Fab Lab](https://fablabbcn.org/)
- **Paris, France** — [The Louvre](https://www.louvre.fr/en), [La Maison des Artistes](https://www.artistes.org/)
- **Austin, USA** — [The Contemporary Austin](https://thecontemporaryaustin.org/), [The Commune](https://www.thecommuneatx.com/)
- **Lisbon, Portugal** — [MAAT](https://www.maat.pt/), [Village Underground](https://www.vulisboa.com/)
- **Tokyo, Japan** — [Mori Art Museum](http://mori.art.museum), [3331 Arts Chiyoda](https://www.3331.jp/en/)
- **Mexico City, Mexico** — [Museo Tamayo](https://museotamayo.org/), [Casa Picnic](https://www.instagram.com/casapicnic/)
- **Amsterdam, Netherlands** — [Rijksmuseum](https://www.rijksmuseum.nl/en/visit), [Trouw](https://www.trouwamsterdam.nl/)
- **Buenos Aires, Argentina** — [Museo Nacional de Bellas Artes](https://bellasartes.gob.ar/), [La Usina del Arte](https://www.lausinadelarte.org.ar/)
- **Melbourne, Australia** — [NGV](https://www.ngv.vic.gov.au/), [Testing Grounds](https://testing-grounds.com/)
- **Copenhagen, Denmark** — [Louisiana Museum](https://louisiana.dk/en/), [Space10](https://space10.com/)
- **Cape Town, South Africa** — [Zeitz MOCAA](https://zeitzmocaa.museum/), [Woodstock Exchange](https://woodstockexchange.co.za/)
- **Seoul, South Korea** — [Leeum Museum](https://leeum.org/), [Seoul Art Space Geumcheon](https://www.transartists.org/en/air/seoul-art-space-geumcheon)
- **Marrakech, Morocco** — [Musée Yves Saint Laurent](https://www.museeyslmarrakech.com/), [Al Maqam](https://www.almamaq.com/)
