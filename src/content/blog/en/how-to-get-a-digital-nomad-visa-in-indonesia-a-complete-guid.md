## As remote work continues to grow, many countries are introducing **digital nomad visas** to attract global talent.

If you're considering moving abroad to work remotely, understanding how to get a **digital nomad visa** is crucial. This guide covers visa requirements, how to apply for a **nomad visa** in Indonesia, and why it’s a top choice for remote workers.

---

## What is a digital nomad visa?

Many digital nomads mistakenly rely on tourist visas, which can lead to fines or deportation. In our recent [LinkedIn post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/slow-working_slowork-connect-work-activity-7334201989754482688-NA3f?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_ios&rcm=ACoAAB7pUTUBmPsASMH0Mkj_M8hNf8c4xQh-Z5o), we explained the difference between tourist visas and nomad visas — and why getting the right one matters.

A **digital nomad visa** allows remote workers to live abroad legally while continuing to work for clients or employers based outside that country. It offers long-term stability and often access to benefits like tax breaks, coworking spaces, or local banking.

---

## Why is a digital nomad visa important?

Tourist visas are often restrictive and can lead to **visa runs** or overstays. A proper **digital nomad visa** allows you to stay longer, work legally, and enjoy access to services like healthcare, housing, or even business setup.

For host countries, these visas attract skilled professionals who contribute economically, making this a win-win for both sides.

## How to apply for Indonesia’s digital nomad visa

Currently, Indonesia doesn’t have a named “digital nomad visa,” but the **B211A visa** serves the same purpose for many nomads.

### ✅ 1. Understand the visa type

- The **B211A socio-cultural visa** allows an initial stay of **60 days**, extendable up to **6 months**.
- Indonesia is considering a future **five-year digital nomad visa**, but it’s not active yet.

### ✅ 2. Check eligibility

- Proof of remote work or freelancing outside Indonesia  
- Valid passport (6+ months)  
- Financial solvency  
- Health/travel insurance  

### ✅ 3. Prepare documents

- Completed visa application  
- Passport photo  
- Bank statements  
- Return flight proof  
- Insurance documents  
- Sponsorship letter (if applicable)

### ✅ 4. Submit your application

You can apply online, via consulates, or through local visa agencies in Bali ([like this one](https://www.balivisa.com/)).

### ✅ 5. Pay the visa fee

Costs range from **$150–250 USD**, depending on processing time and agency services.

### ✅ 6. Receive your visa and enter Indonesia

Once approved, you can begin your stay and extend as needed — but make sure to follow conditions to avoid fines or bans.
